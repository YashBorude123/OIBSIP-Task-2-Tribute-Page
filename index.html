About


Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India.
Kalam had an inspiring life journey that began in a humble background. He graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later specialized in aerospace engineering. He joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and made significant contributions to India's missile and space programs.

Awards

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam received numerous awards and honors throughout his illustrious career. Some of the notable awards and recognitions bestowed upon him include:

1. Bharat Ratna: In 1997, Dr. Kalam was honored with India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, for his exceptional contributions to the field of science and technology.
2.Padma Vibhushan: In 1990, he received the Padma Vibhushan, the second-highest civilian award in India, in recognition of his contributions to the defense and space sectors.
3.Veer Savarkar Award: Dr. Kalam was honored with the Veer Savarkar Award by the Government of India in 1998 for his contributions to the nation.
4.Ramanujan Award: He received the Ramanujan Award in 2000 from the Alwars Research Center, Chennai, for his distinguished contributions to science.
5.King Charles II Medal: In 2007, the Royal Society, UK, awarded Dr. Kalam with the King Charles II Medal for his outstanding contributions to science and engineering.
6.Hoover Medal: In 2009, he became the first Indian to receive the prestigious Hoover Medal from the ASME Foundation, USA, for his outstanding public service.
7.Von Braun Award: Dr. Kalam was honored with the Von Braun Award by the National Space Society, USA, in 2013 for his contributions to space exploration and advocacy.
8.Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration: He received this award in 1997 for promoting national integration and communal harmony.
9.Doctor of Science: Dr. Kalam received honorary doctorates from several esteemed institutions, including the University of Wolverhampton, UK, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, and the University of Waterloo, Canada.
